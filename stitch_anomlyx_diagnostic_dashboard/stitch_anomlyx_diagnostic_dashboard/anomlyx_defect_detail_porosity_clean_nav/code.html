Defect Library chevron_right Porosity

Porosity

A collective term for small cavities, gas pockets, or pores within the metal structure, typically caused by gas entrapment during the solidification process.

X: 124.0 | Y: 88.2
MAG 50X
LOW SEVERITY PINHOLE TYPE

Visual Signs

Sparse, isolated spherical voids < 0.5mm. Surface appears largely continuous with minimal pitting.

Primary Root Cause

Minor atmospheric moisture or slight contamination of surface coating.

Recommended Remedy

  • Surface pre-cleaning
  • Optimize humidity controls
X: 442.1 | Y: 215.7
MAG 25X
MEDIUM SEVERITY GAS POCKET TYPE

Visual Signs

Clustered voids (0.5mm - 1.5mm) in localized areas. Visible disruption of flow lines in the material.

Primary Root Cause

Inadequate venting or excessive moisture in the mold/casting environment.

Recommended Remedy

  • Check vent obstruction
  • Increase pre-heat temperature
  • Check gas shielding flow
X: 981.5 | Y: 412.0
MAG 10X
HIGH SEVERITY BLOWHOLE TYPE

Visual Signs

Large jagged cavities > 2mm. Widespread honeycombing or complete cross-section weakening.

Primary Root Cause

Severe gas contamination, massive vent failure, or incorrect alloy chemistry.

Recommended Remedy

  • Halt production line immediately
  • Full system calibration
  • Alloy purity analysis

Engineering Analysis

Common Causes

Metallurgical Gas

Hydrogen/Nitrogen solubility shifts during cooling phases.

Turbulence

Air entrapment due to high-velocity flow in narrow gates.

Affected Processes

Die Casting Sand Casting MIG/TIG Welding 3D Metal Printing (SLM)

Standard Inspection Notes
